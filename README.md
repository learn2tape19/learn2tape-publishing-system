## How Learn2Tape Publishing Works

### The Learning Architecture

Learn2Tape teaches 12 core competencies across three phases:

1. **Phase I (See Differently):** Challenge assumptions. Readers begin to recognize limitations in their current clinical thinking.
2. **Phase II (Practice Differently):** Develop new skills. Readers integrate evidence-based approaches into established practice.
3. **Phase III (Become Different):** Identity transformation. Readers internalize themselves as clinical thinkers and educators.

### The Publication Model

For each learning objective:

1. **Canonical Expression:** A 1,200–2,000 word blog article teaching the objective
2. **Derivative Assets:** Instagram carousel, Facebook post, LinkedIn article, email, Instagram Stories—all expressing the same learning objective through different formats
3. **Distribution:** Email sequences, social media, search (organic), Brevo automations
4. **Analytics:** Tracking engagement, educational outcomes, and business impact
5. **Iteration:** Using real-world data to inform next cycles

### The Production Workflow

Every piece of content follows a defined workflow:

Idea → Research → Draft → Clinical Review → Editorial Review → SEO → Media → Derivatives → Scheduling → Publishing → Analytics

See [03_CONTENT_WORKFLOW.md](00_PUBLISHING_SYSTEM/03_CONTENT_WORKFLOW.md) for the complete process.

## Founding Resolutions

These principles govern all operational decisions:

### Resolution 001: The System Exists to Accelerate Publishing

The Learn2Tape Publishing System exists to accelerate publishing, not delay it.

No governance document may be expanded unless:
- production exposes a genuine deficiency,
- analytics identify a measurable problem, or
- a new product cannot be supported by the existing system.

Otherwise: **Publish.**

### Resolution 002: Publish Before Perfect

A published educational asset teaches. An unpublished perfect draft teaches no one.

When production reaches the editorial quality defined by the Editorial Charter, it should be published.

Future improvements belong in the next version—not in perpetual revision.
