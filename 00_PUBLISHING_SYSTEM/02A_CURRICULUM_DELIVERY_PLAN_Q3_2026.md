## Purpose

This document schedules how the Learn2Tape Curriculum (02_CURRICULUM_BLUEPRINT.md) is delivered during Q3 2026 (12 weeks, starting July 15, 2026).

This is not a content calendar. It is a production command center. It specifies:
- Which learning objective is taught each week
- What assets must be produced
- What research and stories are required
- What we're measuring
- What the educational outcome should be
- How readers should move to the next step

This document is quarterly and evolves. The curriculum is permanent.

---

## Scope

This document governs:
- Week-by-week curriculum delivery schedule
- Production requirements for each week's learning objective
- Asset readiness assessment
- Educational outcomes and success metrics
- Distribution strategy per week
- CTA strategy per week

This document does NOT govern:
- Whether these learning objectives are correct (see 02_CURRICULUM_BLUEPRINT)
- How to produce each piece (see 03_CONTENT_WORKFLOW)
- Asset standards for each format (see 05_DERIVATIVE_STANDARDS)

## WEEK 1: July 15-21, 2026

### Learning Objective

**Competency 1 — Clinical Reasoning as Foundation**

Clinicians understand that clinical reasoning—not technique—is the foundation of effective taping.

### Canonical Article

**Title:** "Tape Doesn't Fail... Clinical Reasoning Does."

**URL Slug:** `/clinical-reasoning-is-foundation/`

**Word Count:** 1,500-1,800 words

**Structure:** Situation → Common Mistake → Better Approach → Why It Works → Clinical Takeaway → Continue Learning

### Delivery Assets

- **Canonical Blog** — Primary educational expression (published Tuesday 8 AM)
- **Instagram Carousel** — 5 slides (published Tuesday 8 AM)
- **Facebook Post** — Narrative version (published Tuesday 8 AM)
- **LinkedIn Article** — Professional angle (published Tuesday 9 AM)
- **Newsletter Email** — Educational message (sent Tuesday 8 AM via Brevo)
- **Instagram Stories** — Quick teaser (published Tuesday 8 AM)
- **Brevo Automation** — Assigned to Awareness funnel stage

### Primary Educational Outcome

After reading, the clinician understands:
- Technique without reasoning is unreliable
- Clinical reasoning is learnable (not just a function of experience)
- Their clinical thinking is the foundation of their results

### Belief Shift

**Before:** "Taping is a technique I apply."
**After:** "Taping is the expression of my clinical reasoning."

### Clinical Competency Target

The clinician can explain why they chose a specific taping approach based on their assessment (not habit or intuition).

### Assessment Prompt

**Before:** "When should I tape?"
**After:** "What assessment findings would make taping appropriate here?"

### CTA Strategy

**Primary CTA:** Read the next article in the series (stay engaged)
**Secondary CTA:** Join the email list (ongoing education)
**No Enrollment CTA Yet** (we're in Awareness phase)

### Production Requirements

**Research Needed:**
- Studies on clinical decision-making in manual therapy
- Evidence on how clinical reasoning improves outcomes
- Research on intuition vs. structured decision-making

**Stories Required:**
- One patient case where clinical reasoning changed the outcome
- One mistake Drew made by skipping assessment
- One experience of learning to trust reasoning over experience

**Images Required:**
- Featured image (1200x628): clinician assessing, not just taping
- 2-3 in-article images showing assessment thinking
- Carousel images (1080x1350 each): conceptual + clinical examples

**Testimonials/Quotes:**
- One quote from a clinician on the power of clinical reasoning
- One patient perspective on feeling understood (not just treated)

**Clinical Examples:**
- One common scenario where intuition fails
- One scenario where structured reasoning succeeded
- Decision framework showing the difference

### Analytics Focus

**Measure For Awareness Phase:**
- Blog traffic (organic)
- Email open rate (first impression)
- Time on page (engagement depth)
- Scroll depth (do they read the whole article?)

**Target Benchmarks:**
- Email open rate: 30%+
- Email CTR: 20%+
- Avg. time on page: 3+ minutes
- Scroll to 75%+: 40%+

**Success Definition:** Readers engage deeply enough to understand the belief shift.

### Brevo Funnel Assignment

- **List:** L2T - Newsletter (all new subscribers)
- **Stage:** Awareness (first article in journey)
- **Automation:** Auto-trigger welcome email pointing to this article

### Production Deadline

- Research complete: July 8
- First draft: July 10
- Clinical review: July 11
- Final revisions: July 12
- Derivatives ready: July 13
- Scheduled for publish: July 14 (publish Tuesday July 15, 8 AM)

### Notes

This is **Blog #1**. It establishes the voice and proves the workflow. Every subsequent week follows the same process but with compressed timeline (should be 5-7 days total, not 7).
