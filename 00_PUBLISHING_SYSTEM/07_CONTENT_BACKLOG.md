## Purpose

This document is a living repository of content ideas organized by learning objective and status.

The Content Backlog captures ideas as they arise, preventing good thoughts from being lost and eliminating "what should I write?" decision fatigue.

---

## Scope

This document governs:
- How ideas are captured
- How ideas are organized and prioritized
- How ideas flow from Backlog to Editorial Calendar
- How ideas are tracked through production and publication

This document does NOT govern:
- Whether an idea gets published (see 01_EDITORIAL_CHARTER)
- Which idea gets published when (see 02A_CURRICULUM_DELIVERY_PLAN)
- How to produce the idea (see 03_CONTENT_WORKFLOW)

---

## Core Principle: Capture Everything, Prioritize Ruthlessly

**Capture:** Any idea that might become content gets written down. No idea is too rough.

**Organize:** All ideas are mapped to learning objectives.

**Prioritize:** Only ideas that serve the curriculum and have supporting assets get scheduled.

This prevents analysis paralysis: "What should I write?" → Check the backlog. "Should I write this?" → Does it serve a learning objective? Do we have supporting assets?

---

## How to Add to the Backlog

### When an Idea Strikes

**Capture immediately:**
- What's the core idea?
- Which learning objective does it serve?
- Why does it matter?
- Any initial thoughts on approach?

**Example:**
"Patient came in assuming ankle was torn. Assessment showed it wasn't. This illustrates why assessment matters more than intuition. → LO02 (Assessment as Foundation). Good story. Add to backlog."

### Where to Capture

- **Preferred:** This backlog document (organized)
- **Acceptable:** Phone voice memo transcribed later
- **Acceptable:** Notebook, then transcribed

**Never:** Only in a chat or email where it can be forgotten

### Format

Add a new entry (see template below) with:
- Unique ID (LO##_B[backlog number])
- Topic/title
- Why it matters (2–3 sentences)
- Learning objective it serves
- Curriculum phase
- Source (where the idea came from)
- Evidence availability (do we have research? stories? examples?)
- Status (Captured, Researching, Ready, Scheduled, In Production, Published, or Deferred)

## Content Backlog Status Definitions

### CAPTURED

Idea is written down but not yet researched. No timeline commitment.

**Next step:** When you have time, research if supporting assets exist.

**Stay in this status for:** 1–4 weeks

### RESEARCHING

Active research into supporting assets. Gathering stories, evidence, and examples.

**Next step:** Complete research; move to READY or DEFERRED.

**Typical timeline:** 2–3 weeks of background work

### READY

Idea is fully researched, supporting assets identified, ready to schedule.

**Next step:** Schedule into Editorial Calendar when slot opens.

**Backlog contains:** 3–5 READY ideas at any time

### SCHEDULED

Idea is assigned to a specific week in the Editorial Calendar.

**Next step:** Begin production on assigned week.

**Timeline:** Moves to IN PRODUCTION when drafting starts

### IN PRODUCTION

Article is actively being written, reviewed, and produced.

**Next step:** Publication.

**Timeline:** 5–10 days (per Content Workflow)

### PUBLISHED

Article has been published and distributed.

**Tracking:** Update date published; ensure analytics are set up.

**Backlog stays:** As historical record of what was published

### DEFERRED

Idea isn't ready to publish; reasons noted.

**Why ideas are deferred:**
- Insufficient evidence for claims
- Needed assets aren't available
- Topic doesn't serve current curriculum
- Better timing makes sense later
- Audience feedback suggests low relevance

**Next step:** Either revive with new evidence, or archive if no longer relevant.

**Timeline:** Review deferred ideas quarterly; revive or archive

## Process: How Ideas Move Through the Backlog

### Weekly Backlog Review (Friday)

1. Any new ideas captured this week?
2. Any CAPTURED ideas ready to research?
3. Any RESEARCHING ideas ready to schedule?
4. Are we on track for next week's SCHEDULED article?

### Monthly Backlog Review (End of Month)

1. Which READY ideas should we prioritize for next quarter?
2. Any CAPTURED ideas that should be DEFERRED?
3. Any ideas missing that we should capture?
4. Update analytics for PUBLISHED articles

### Quarterly Backlog Strategic Review (End of Quarter)

1. Which learning objectives need more backlog ideas?
2. What patterns in idea quality or topic resonance?
3. Should we adjust editorial strategy based on what's in backlog?
4. Revive or archive DEFERRED ideas based on evidence?
