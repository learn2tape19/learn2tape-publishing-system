## Purpose

This document organizes all research, stories, images, evidence, and supporting assets for the Learn2Tape Curriculum by learning objective.

When you begin writing an article, you refer to the corresponding learning objective here. Every supporting asset is listed, scored for readiness, and organized for immediate access.

This prevents hunting for resources and ensures consistency across the curriculum.

---

## Scope

This document governs:
- Organization of all supporting assets by learning objective
- Asset readiness scoring (0-100% for each objective)
- Evidence and research libraries
- Story and testimonial collections
- Image and visual asset inventory
- Clinical examples and case studies
- Tools and downloadables

This document does NOT govern:
- How to write articles (see 03_CONTENT_WORKFLOW)
- Derivative format standards (see 05_DERIVATIVE_STANDARDS)
- Production timeline (see 02A_CURRICULUM_DELIVERY_PLAN)

---

## How to Use This Document

**Before writing an article:**

1. Find the learning objective
2. Check overall readiness score
3. Review all supporting assets already available
4. Identify which assets need to be created/refined
5. Allocate production time accordingly

**Readiness Score Interpretation:**

- **90-100%:** Ready to write immediately; all assets available
- **75-89%:** Good to write; minor asset gaps manageable
- **60-74%:** Can write; allocate extra time for asset creation
- **Below 60%:** Delay production until assets are ready

## COMPETENCY 1: Clinical Reasoning as Foundation

**Learning Objective:** Clinicians understand that clinical reasoning—not technique—is the foundation of effective taping.

**Readiness Score: 83%**

### Research Library

**Status: 85% Complete**

- [ ] Ericsson, A. K. (2008). "Deliberate Practice and the Acquisition and Maintenance of Expert Performance in Medicine and Related Domains." *Academic Medicine*, 83(10 Suppl), S52-S65. **Status: Available**
  - Key insight: Expertise is built through deliberate practice and decision-making, not just experience
  - Relevance: Supports belief shift that reasoning can be learned

- [ ] Atkinson, R. C., & Shiffrin, R. M. (1968). "Human Memory: A Proposed System and Its Control Processes." *The Psychology of Learning and Motivation*, 2, 89-195. **Status: Available**
  - Key insight: Working memory constraints mean efficient reasoning is more important than experience
  - Relevance: Explains why structure beats intuition

- [ ] Kahneman, D., & Tversky, A. (1974). "Judgment Under Uncertainty: Heuristics and Biases." *Science*, 185(4157), 1124-1131. **Status: Available**
  - Key insight: Intuition and experience can mislead; structured processes reduce bias
  - Relevance: Core evidence for reasoning over intuition

- [ ] Klein, G. (2015). "Seeing What Others Don't: The Remarkable Ways We Gain Insights." *PublicAffairs*. **Status: Available**
  - Key insight: Expert intuition is based on patterns recognized through practice with feedback
  - Relevance: Pattern recognition requires reflection, not just experience

- [ ] Schön, D. A. (1983). "The Reflective Practitioner: How Professionals Think in Action." *Basic Books*. **Status: Partially Available**
  - Key insight: Professional learning happens through reflection on practice
  - Relevance: Reasoning can be taught through reflection framework
  - Gap: Need practical examples of reflection in clinical context

## Asset Readiness Scoring Explained

### 90-100%: Ready to Write

All critical assets present. Article can be written without delays. Supporting materials (images, templates) may still be in progress but won't delay publication.

### 75-89%: Good to Write

Most critical assets available. Some gaps (typically images or secondary testimonials) can be created during writing timeline.

### 60-74%: Can Write with Allocation

Core research and stories available, but significant production work needed. Allocate extra time (1-2 days) for asset creation.

### Below 60%: Delay Recommended

Critical gaps exist. Investing in asset development now saves emergency production time later.

## How to Use This Document During Production

**Step 1: Identify Learning Objective**
Find the week's competency in this document.

**Step 2: Check Readiness Score**
- 75%+: Proceed to writing
- 60-74%: Allocate extra production time
- Below 60%: Create missing assets first

**Step 3: Review All Assets**
Use the structured list to understand what's available:
- Research: What evidence supports this?
- Stories: Which personal examples are available?
- Examples: What clinical scenarios demonstrate the principle?
- Tools: What can be provided to readers?

**Step 4: Identify Gaps**
Note what's missing. During writing, allocate time to create it:
- New interviews needed?
- Photos need scheduling?
- Templates need design?

**Step 5: Create Missing Assets**
Before finishing the article, produce any critical gaps.
